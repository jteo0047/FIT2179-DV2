§ 02 — National Pulse

Tourism's Long Road Back: 2017–2024

Total visitors and expenditure collapsed during the pandemic, then staged a remarkable multi-year recovery. By 2024, both metrics had surpassed pre-pandemic peaks, with expenditure reaching RM106.7 billion — a new all-time high.

Total domestic visitor-trips & expenditure, 2017–2024

Source: Tourism Malaysia, Jadual 1 (2024 edition)
Record-breaking 2024: At RM106.7 billion, total tourism expenditure in 2024 exceeded the previous 2019 peak of RM103.2 billion for the first time since the pandemic — a 25.7% jump from 2023 alone.

§ 03 — State Rankings

How State Rankings Shifted: 2019 → 2024

Some states outpaced the national recovery while others slipped in the rankings. Melaka climbed dramatically — from 8th in 2019 to 4th in 2024 — while Sabah and Perlis also gained ground. Kedah and Perak fell back.

State rank by domestic visitor arrivals, 2019 versus 2024

§ 04 — Spending Patterns

Where the Money Goes

Shopping tops the list of tourist expenditure at 37.4% of the total in 2024. Looking back to 2019, fuel's share climbed during the recovery years while accommodation spending contracted — reflecting how travel behaviour shifted as Malaysians resumed domestic trips post-pandemic.

§ 08 — Who Travels & Why

Purpose of Visit & Visitor Demographics
